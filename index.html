Biography

Michael Spence is an American poet who drove public-transit buses in the Seattle area for thirty years, retiring on February 14, 2014. His city-level vantage—engines humming, neon on rain—shapes much of his work. Before his transit career, Spence earned a B.A. in English from the University of Washington and served as a junior officer aboard the USS John F. Kennedy (CV‑67).

Published Works

  • The Spine (1987) — recognized in the Walt Whitman Award competition
  • Adam Chooses (1998)
  • Crush Depth (2009)
  • The Bus Driver’s Threnody (2014) — finalist, The New Criterion Poetry Prize
  • Umbilical (2016) — winner, The New Criterion Poetry Prize

Poetic Themes

Spence’s poetry often captures the transient beauty of daily encounters—passengers boarding a late‑night bus, the hum of the engine beneath quiet contemplation, and echoes of history intertwined with the present.

Recognition

He received a Creative Writing Fellowship from the National Endowment for the Arts in 1990. His work has appeared in journals including The Hudson Review and North American Review. Umbilical has been described as “an extraordinary collection of poetry, filled with reflection, understanding, generosity, and not a little love.”
